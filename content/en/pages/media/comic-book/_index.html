Graphic novels are a fantastic way to make the debate about degrowth and economics accessible to climate activists, teachers, students, workers, parents, and anyone else who isn’t a classical economist but wants to help stop climate collapse. This page displays several comic books you can share with friends and family or read yourself! ‘Who Is Afraid of Degrowth’ is particularly focused on degrowth. It contains quotes from a wide range of experts woven into an engaging narrative that invites you to explore and join this crucial debate about economics, ecology, democracy, human rights, technology, and our future.

body: >-


"Degrowth is not only possible and necessary; it entails all kinds of good stuff you probably haven’t heard about. It is also, despite all claims to the contrary, something that cannot be done to people but can only be truly achieved by a mass movement of the people. ‘Who Is Afraid of Degrowth’ offers a new way to learn about degrowth and join the most important debate of our time."

Download the Free PDF Here!
https://www.celinekeller.com/who-is-afraid-of-degrowth


This graphic novel was created by Celine Keller . A brilliant artist who uses her skills, and as much time and energy as she can, on projects that promote social, climate and ecological justice. Her website hosts various other free comics in English and German about subjects like the Energy Charter Treaty, the Discourses of Climate delay and more. You should 100%  follow her . 


"What if the creators of these [manga] universes were the new sensei (masters) of the post-capitalist utopia? This is the hypothesis of this work: young people find in mangas and anime a radical critique of the ecological and social excesses of capitalism and a source of revolutionary energy capable of overturning the system to build a desirable future. Gabriel Malek brings to light the main ideological lines of manga, and offers us a good reason to reconsider these revolutionary stories calling for a new cooperation between humans as well as with all life."

Check it out here!
https://www.payot-rivages.fr/payot/livre/les-sensei-de-la-d%C3%A9croissance-9782228935623


"A murderous Bambi or a Nazi teddy bear are some of the characters that inhabit the strange vignettes of Miguel Brieva, one of the protagonists of the new generation of Spanish comics. His absurd and cruel world is a reflection of our current consumerist, standardised and uncritical society. An extremely original, lucid and radical graphic work, presented in alphabetical order in the manner of an encyclopaedia."

See More Here!
https://www.novedadescomics.com/comic-europeo/bienvenido-al-mundo-enciclopedia-clismon-79756.html


"World Without End" is the fruit of a years-long collaboration between Christophe Blain and Jean-Marc Jancovici where they explore our dependence on fossil fuels, the profound changes our planet is undergoing, and the resulting consequences for society as a whole. The dream of endless economic growth might be an illusion... but that doesn't mean we are doomed to destruction, if we take the necessary steps today. A candid and empathetic analysis that leaves readers with a better understanding of today's world and where we go from here as a society. (Available in English and French)

See More Here!
https://books.google.com/books/about/World_Without_End.html?id=vxF1EAAAQBAJ&redir_esc=y


Of course, nothing is perfect. We therefore invite you to read this critique of the techno-optimism presented in World Without End.
